Students passing SSC or equivalent examination are eligible to secure admission for FYJC.

Other Boards:

Means the Education Boards other than the State Board viz. - CBSE, ICSE, IGCSE, IB, NIOS or Boards of other States.

  • Students of other boards (Other than Maharashtra State Board) who want to take admission in a Junior College affiliated to Maharashtra State Board within any online admission area should fill online form and participate in this admission process.
  • After entering the examination seat number, the other board student’s information will not be filled automatically as data of such students is not available. These students have to fill in their personal information themselves carefully and get the form approved from the nearest Guidance centre.
  • All students other than Maharashtra Board should approve their online application form from the nearest Guidance Centre. (If the facility is not available in their own school.)
  • If applicants result of Std. X is in Grade form, students have to get the grades converted into marks from their secondary (10th ) School Principal or as per the guidelines of Deputy Director Office. And after that only such students will be able to fill Part-2 of the application form.
  • Eligibility Certificate - Students who have passed 10th exam from any other board need to get eligibility certificate from the state board after admission. An application for eligibility (available with respective Junior College) is required to be submitted along with necessary documents.
  • Other board’s students can participate in this admission process with their provisional marksheet. However they need to submit original copy of marksheet while confirming their admission in the Junior College.
  •  For CBSE students first five subjects only will be considered.
  •  For ICSE students who have passed out with 6 subjects for them from Group I and Group II any five subjects can be considered.
  •  For ICSE students who have passed out with 7 subjects for them there are two options that can be considered.
    • Option – 1: Best of five subjects from Group I & Group II (out of 600) can be considered.
    • Option – 2: Total average of 700 can be considered i.e. Group I, II & III together.

  • Admission process to F.Y.J.C. has been made online by the Govt. of Maharashtra from the academic year 2009-2010 vide GRM/HSC170944/09 U MASH I dt.28-06- 2009.
  • Gujarati Linguistic Minority quota admissions will be on-line.
  • As our institution has been conferred with the Gujarati Linguistic Minority Status by the Govt. of Maharashtra, 50% of the seats will be reserved for the aforesaid Linguistic Minority.
  • Minority admission is as per the guidelines given by Education Department. Admission is strictly as per merit list prepared by the College administration.
  • Second language – Due to limited seats, students may have to accept any other language offered by the College other than the desired languages.
  • Commerce stream – For optional subjects (Mathematics / Sec Practice) admission is strictly based on the percentage of marks secured in Mathematics at 10th Std. level.
  • Final decision regarding admission will rest at the absolute discretion of the College administration and management.

Project / Earthquake Affected

Certificate issued by Collector/District Rehabilitation officer stating that project affected or earthquake affected or dependent need to be attached/to avail reservation under this category

Wards of the Employees of State Government/Central Government/Private Sector

Coming on transfer in the online admission area - Original Certificate showing transfer of the employee from outside to the online admission area on or after 1st October of the last year need to be submitted while taking admission . Further, the ward must have passed Std. X or equivalent examination from a school outside of the online admission area.

Wife or Wards of Servicemen and Ex-Servicemen

  • For Serviceman: Service Certificate.
  • For Ex-Serviceman: Discharge Certificate or Certificate issued by District Sainik Board.

Wards of Freedom Fighters

Applicants who are dependent on the freedom fighters and are so nominated by the freedom fighters, must submit a certificate issued by the District Collector to that effect.
